Snoek Braai

This dish is one that is great for a cool summer evening. It's one that is great for winding down on a Friday after a long week, or even something to enjoy with your friends during a weekend get-together! Snoek is a type of fish typically caught around the Western Cape coastal areas. Although it is rather boney, the flavour is unbeatable and I find it to be rather refreshing, especially with a dash of lemon !!

Ingredients:

  • 1 Medium Sized Fresh Snoek
  • 3 Tablespoons Butter
  • 3 Tablespoons Apricot Jam
  • 1/2 Fresh Lemon
  • 1 Teaspoon Salt
  • 1 Teaspoon Ground Pepper

Method:

  1. Ensure the snoek is cleaned and gutted so that it can easily fold open.
  2. Melt the butter.
  3. Evenly spread the butter over the fish so that it can absorb into the fish.
  4. Melt the jam until fully liquid and gently smear the jam on top of the butter, over the fish.
  5. Gently squeeze some fresh lemon juice on top of the butter and jam, ensuring small droplets are spread evenly.
  6. Place the fish in tin foil and ensure the fish is fully covered on both sides.
  7. Allow 4 to 5 hours to allow the butter, jam, and lemon to draw into the fish
  8. Ensure your coals are not too hot as it will burn the fish.(coals should ideally be at a temperature where you can put your hand relatively close, and not feel much burn)
  9. Cook each side for around 15 to 20 minutes (remember:the snoek cooks inside its own juices inside the foil)
  10. Before serving, lightly season with some salt and pepper.
  11. Last and most importantly, ENJOY!!!

Now that you have your refreshing and ultra-delicious Snoek, be sure to enjoy it thoroughly, and why crack open a cold one while you're at it!!
