Milktart Recipe

This dish is one that you'll never forget the taste of. It's just that good! It has a sweet crust and a light, fluffy and sweet custard-esque filling.This dish is one that will fill your kitchen with sweet aromas never to be forgotten. Milktart is best served warm or at room temperature with a light dusting of cinnamon powder and paired a with a strong black coffee on a Sunday afternoon. Just be sure to grab a slice before everybody else finishes it before you do!!

Ingredients:

  • Cooking Spray
  • 1 Cup White Sugar
  • 3 Tablespoons Butter, Melted
  • 3 Large Eggs (separated)
  • 1 Cup Cake Flour
  • 1 Teaspoon Baking Flour
  • 1/4 Teaspoon Salt
  • 4 Cups Milk
  • 1 Teaspoon Vanilla Extract
  • 1 Tablespoon Cinnamon Sugar

Method:

  1. Preheat the oven to 375 degrees F (190 degrees C).
  2. Lightly coat a 9-inch deep-dish pie plate with a cooking spray.
  3. Mix together sugar and melted butter in a large bowl until smooth. Add egg yolks and beat until and fluffy. Sift in cake flour, baking powder, and salt. Stir until the mixture is well blended. Mix in milk and vanilla until combined.
  4. Whip egg whites with an electric mixer in a separate bowl to stiff peaks. Fold into the batter. Pour into the prepared pie plate. Sprinkle cinnamon sugar on top.
  5. Bake in the preheated oven for 25 minutes, then reduce the temperature to 325 degrees F (165 degrees C). Continue baking until the center is set when the pie is gently jiggled, around 25 to 30 minutes.
  6. Allow to cool to room temperature, and serve.
  7. The final step, and most important, ENJOY!!!

Now that you have your delicious milktart, be sure to enjoy it thoroughly, and be prepared to make one again soon, as the whole neighborhood will want a slice!
